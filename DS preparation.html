03

Interview tips — senior level

Lead with trade-offs, not answers

Interviewers at senior level expect you to discuss why you chose one approach over another. Don't just say "I used XGBoost" — explain why over a neural network for this specific problem.

Use real project examples

For every concept, prepare a 1-minute story from your own work. "At my last role, we had a class imbalance problem and I chose…" beats a textbook answer every time.

Don't skip baselines

Always mention that you start with a simple baseline (logistic regression, majority class) before building complex models. This signals engineering maturity.

Production > notebooks

For 10 yrs experience, expect deep questions on deployment, monitoring, and drift. Candidates who only know Jupyter tend to fail senior interviews.

Talk about failures honestly

Senior interviewers want to hear about a model that failed in production. Prepare a STAR story: what went wrong, how you diagnosed it, what you changed, what you learned.

Statistical rigor matters

Many DS candidates underestimate stats questions. Be ready to explain p-values, confidence intervals, and A/B test design from first principles — not just how to run them.

GenAI is expected now

Even if your background is classical ML, know the basics of transformers, embeddings, RAG, and prompt engineering. These appear in senior interviews even for non-NLP roles.

Business impact over accuracy

When asked about a project, lead with business value ("reduced churn by 18%") before technical metrics. Senior roles require you to connect models to outcomes stakeholders care about.
